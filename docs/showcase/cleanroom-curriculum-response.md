# Cleanroom Operations Technician Curriculum Discovery

This first-draft response supports the internal `/dashboard/showcase` walkthrough. It demonstrates the analysis method and curriculum shape; site procedures and subject-matter experts must validate all production content.

## 1. Requirement Gathering & Analysis Plan

### Objective

Translate the technician role into observable capabilities, learning interventions, and credible evidence rather than building a broad course catalog.

### Discovery

1. Parse the job description into responsibilities, decisions, and expected behaviors.
2. Interview operations supervisors, quality, EHS, experienced technicians, and training owners.
3. Observe cleanroom entry, wafer movement, MES transactions, and alarm response.
4. Review approved SOPs, work instructions, alarm matrices, deviation records, and qualification checklists.
5. Rank tasks by frequency, consequence of error, and need for supervised demonstration.
6. Separate knowledge instruction from hands-on qualification.
7. Validate the map and evidence criteria with site SMEs.
8. Pilot with representative technicians and revise from observed performance.

### Selection Method

The showcase selects three areas that represent distinct operational demands:

| Skill area | Direct job-description responsibility | Curriculum interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cleanroom & Contamination Control | Follow cleanroom protocols and maintain contamination-control standards | Enter correctly, recognize contamination risk, and escalate compromised conditions |
| Wafer Handling & MES Lot Tracking | Handle wafers and use MES to track lots through production | Protect wafers while preserving lot identity, status, and traceability |
| Alarm Response & Escalation | Respond to equipment alarms and escalate issues according to procedure | Recognize alarms, protect work in process, document, and escalate |

The responsibility statements above are direct requirements from the supplied job description. The curriculum interpretations translate those requirements into observable behaviors; site SMEs must confirm the exact procedure and evidence standard.

### Direct Requirements vs. Assumptions

| Area | Direct from job description | Assumption requiring SME validation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cleanroom & Contamination Control | Cleanroom protocols and contamination control are technician responsibilities | Exact gowning sequence, contamination-response steps, documentation threshold, and supervisor sign-off criteria |
| Wafer Handling & MES Lot Tracking | Wafer handling and MES lot tracking are technician responsibilities | Carrier rules, MES transaction names, hold and mismatch states, and which transactions require observation |
| Alarm Response & Escalation | Equipment-alarm response and escalation are technician responsibilities | Alarm classes, safe-state actions, documentation rules, escalation timing, and technician authority boundaries |

## 2. Training Program Outline / Syllabus

### Cleanroom & Contamination Control

**Outcome:** Protect the controlled environment while entering, working, and responding to contamination risk.

**Source trace:** Job-description responsibility to follow cleanroom protocols and maintain contamination-control standards.

- Cleanroom Entry, Gowning, and Contamination Response
- Cleanroom Behavior and Contamination Risks
- Material Entry and Exit Fundamentals

**Evidence:** Sequencing and decision checks followed by supervisor observation against the approved site checklist.

### Wafer Handling & MES Lot Tracking

**Outcome:** Handle wafers safely while preserving lot identity, status, and traceability.

**Source trace:** Job-description responsibility to handle wafers and use MES to track lots through production.

- Safe Wafer and Carrier Handling
- MES Lot Identification and Transaction Accuracy
- Hold, Mismatch, and Traceability Response

**Evidence:** Handling scenarios, a simulated MES transaction, and observed execution in the approved environment.

### Alarm Response & Escalation

**Outcome:** Recognize abnormal conditions, place work in the appropriate state, and escalate correctly.

**Source trace:** Job-description responsibility to respond to equipment alarms and escalate according to procedure.

- Alarm Recognition and Immediate Response
- Stop, Safe-State, and Escalation Decisions
- Event Documentation and Handoff

**Evidence:** Timed decisions, documentation practice, and supervisor verification using the site alarm matrix.

### First Capability Card

**Cleanroom Entry, Gowning, and Contamination Response** is the first card because it is foundational, visible, and easy to connect to both knowledge and observed performance.

The card includes:

1. The concept of a site-approved gowning sequence.
2. A contamination-risk decision after contact with an uncontrolled surface.
3. A documentation and escalation decision when product impact may exist.
4. A required supervisor observation before the capability can be marked Ready.

The production card must reproduce the approved local SOP rather than generic gowning guidance.

## 3. Justification and Confidence

### Rationale

The selected areas provide a focused curriculum slice across contamination control, product traceability, and abnormal-response behavior. Together they demonstrate how myLearning connects short instruction to decisions, evidence, and supervisor verification.

### Confidence

- **Cleanroom & Contamination Control: High.** Directly grounded in the cleanroom-protocol responsibility. The exact sequence, response, documentation, and verification rules require the site SOP.
- **Wafer Handling & MES Lot Tracking: Medium.** Directly grounded in the wafer-handling and MES responsibility. Terminology, transactions, and exceptions depend on the local MES and fab workflow.
- **Alarm Response & Escalation: Medium.** Directly grounded in the alarm-response responsibility. Correct actions depend on the approved alarm matrix and authority boundaries.

### Explicitly Deferred

- Full tool preventive-maintenance support
- Hazardous gas and chemical-handling depth
- Automated SCORM or Rise conversion
- Production readiness scoring

### Validation Needed

- Approved gowning, contamination-response, and material-transfer procedures
- MES lot states, transactions, holds, and exception handling
- Alarm classifications, safe-state actions, and escalation paths
- Supervisor qualification checklists and evidence-retention rules

The showcase uses capability-level statuses only: **Ready**, **Needs verification**, and **Overdue**. Completion alone does not produce Ready when observed performance is required.
